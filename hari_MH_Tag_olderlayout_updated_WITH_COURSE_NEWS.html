Featured Project: Amrita Kripa Platform

Amrita Dynamic Regional Landslide Risk Management Platform

Status: Launched for Pilot Implementation
Location: Kodagu District, Karnataka
Operational Partner: Karnataka State Natural Disaster Monitoring Centre (KSNDMC)

Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham has launched the Amrita Dynamic Regional Landslide Risk Management Platform (Amrita Kripa), an integrated, real-time decision-support system aimed at strengthening landslide monitoring, preparedness, and emergency response.

The platform has been rolled out in Kodagu District, Karnataka as a pilot deployment and shared with KSNDMC for operational testing. It integrates landslide susceptibility mapping, village-level social vulnerability and exposure, and real-time as well as antecedent rainfall threshold monitoring to deliver hourly updated landslide risk assessments and planning support through an operational dashboard.

Figure: Proposed Dynamic Landslide Early Warning Framework underpinning the Amrita Kripa Platform.

Platform Framework (Work Packages)

WP1: Susceptibility Mapping

Develops a high-resolution baseline Landslide Susceptibility Map (LSM) using geospatial datasets and landslide inventories.

WP2: Social Vulnerability & Exposure

Maps village-level social vulnerability and exposure by integrating demographic and infrastructure indicators to identify the most at-risk communities.

WP3: Real-Time & Antecedent Rainfall Thresholds

Monitors exceedance of established rainfall thresholds under real-time and antecedent conditions to assess landslide triggering potential.

WP4: Dynamic Risk & Response Planning

Integrates hazard and vulnerability layers into hourly updated risk maps to support evacuation planning, shelter identification, and resource allocation via a live dashboard.

Operational Dashboard Features

The platform dashboard supports real-time situational awareness and response planning, including:

  • Realtime Risk View: Displays current rainfall deviation and risk levels.
  • Incident Analysis Widget: Supports event simulation with a buffer (up to 20 km) to identify exposed population and critical infrastructure.
  • Antecedent Analysis: Evaluates saturation and triggering potential across multiple antecedent durations (e.g., 1–15 days).
